The '''ergosphere''' is a place just outside the [[event horizon]] of a [[black hole]]. Its name was given by Remo Ruffini and [[John Archibald Wheeler]] in 1971 and is gotten from the Greek word ἔργον (ergon), which means "work". It got its name because it is possible to get [[energy]] and even [[mass]] from this place. In this place, it's impossible to stay in one place because of "[[frame-dragging]]." According to [[general relativity]], anything that spins will twist the [[spacetime]] around it. This is frame dragging. So, near a [[Rotation|rotating]] black hole, things start spin around it. For a spinning black hole, frame-dragging is so strong that to stay in one place, you'd have to move backward faster than the [[speed of light]], which is impossible.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Spacetime and geometry: an introduction to general relativity|last=Carroll|first=Sean M.|date=1973|publisher=Addison-Wesley|isbn=978-0-8053-8732-2|edition=Nachdr.|location=San Francisco Munich}}</ref>
